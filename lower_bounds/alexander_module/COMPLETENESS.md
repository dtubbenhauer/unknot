# Complete finite-field Alexander-module scan

## Outcome

The finite-field method can be made exhaustive.  Doing so adds thirteen
genuinely new certificates to the paper: eleven new exact values `u=2` and
two improvements from `[1,3]` to `[2,3]`.

The corrected Alexander-module totals are:

- 362 genuinely new lower-bound improvements;
- 303 exact values `u=2`;
- 14 exact values `u=3`;
- 45 further lower-bound improvements.

Combining these with the other two methods in the draft, and retaining the
single overlap at `13a4877`, changes the main totals from 361 exact plus 48
further improvements to **372 exact plus 50 further improvements**, or 422
distinct improvements in total.

## The finite-field index

Let `Lambda=Z[t,t^{-1}]`, let `A_K` be the Alexander module, and put

```
b(K)=max_m dim_{k(m)} A_K tensor_Lambda k(m),
```

where the maximum is over the maximal ideals of `Lambda`.  Every maximal
ideal is of the form `(p,h(t))`, with `p` prime and `h` irreducible over
`F_p`, so `b(K)` is exactly the largest nullity among all finite-field
specializations.

If `M(t)` is an `m` by `m` presentation and `E_r(M)` is generated by its
`(m-r+1)`-minors, then

```
b(K)=max { r : E_r(M) is not Lambda }.
```

Thus the complete finite-field calculation is a finite elementary-ideal
calculation; it does not require an unbounded enumeration of fields.

## Prime and extension bounds

For a fixed integral presentation, the maximum nullity in characteristic
`p` agrees with the maximum nullity in characteristic zero for all but
finitely many primes.  The exceptional primes are effective.  For each
determinantal ideal, take the gcd `g` of its minors over `Q[t]`, divide the
minors by `g`, and clear denominators in a Bezout relation among the
quotients.  Every exceptional prime divides the resulting nonzero integer,
together with the finitely many coefficients whose reduction can change the
support of `g`.

If `M(t)=tV-V^T` and a specialization has nullity at least `r`, the minimal
polynomial of the specialization parameter divides every
`(m-r+1)`-minor.  At least one such minor is nonzero because `M(1)=V-V^T`
is unimodular.  Consequently the required extension degree satisfies

```
degree <= m-r+1.
```

There is no prime bound depending only on the genus.  For any odd prime `q`,

```
V_q = [[q,(q-1)/2],[(q+1)/2,q]]
```

is a genus-one Seifert matrix.  In characteristic `q`, the specialization at
`t=-1` is the zero matrix, while no characteristic-zero specialization has
nullity two.  Thus the exceptional prime can be arbitrarily large at genus
one.

A bound depending only on the crossing number does exist: an `n`-crossing
diagram gives a degree-one Alexander presentation of size at most `n-1`
with uniformly bounded coefficients.  Resultant and Hadamard bounds then
give an explicit, although uselessly large, bound of size
`exp(O(n^2 log n))` for the exceptional primes.

The database matrices themselves are not minimal-genus matrices: among the
5,121 gapped rows their sizes reach 62, and 3,558 matrices are larger than
the crossing number.  Exact unit-pivot reductions shrink every one of them
to size at most 6.

## Relation with the Nakanishi index

Let `e(K)` be the Nakanishi index.  The Forster--Swan theorem gives

```
b(K) <= e(K) <= b(K)+1.
```

Indeed, the Alexander module is torsion over the two-dimensional ring
`Lambda`, so every prime in its support has quotient dimension at most one;
the local generator numbers are bounded by `b(K)`.

The possible gap is real.  For `9_38`, the second elementary ideal is the
unit ideal, so every finite-field specialization has nullity at most one,
whereas Kearton and Wilson proved that its Nakanishi index is two.  Therefore
no cutoff in primes or extension degrees can by itself compute the exact
Nakanishi index in general.  After computing `b(K)`, only the global question
whether the module is `b(K)`-generated remains.

## Thirteen new certificates

Exact `u=2`:

```
13n2301, 13n3613, 13n3715, 13n4107, 13n4142, 13n4274,
13n4472, 13n4474, 13n4649, 13n4657, 13n4957.
```


Eight certificates use `F_16`, four use `F_49`, and one uses `F_81`.  Every
positive certificate with finite-field index at least two in the complete
scan (606 rows) was independently checked by direct elimination in the
original, unreduced Seifert matrix.
